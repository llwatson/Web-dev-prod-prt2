Quinoa & Spinach-Stuffed Peppers With Cashew cream

Serves 4

Recipe (Loma Linda, California) from "Eat Better Live Longer" By: Melissa Petitto, R.D., pg.43

Ingredients

Cashew Cream:

  • 1/2 cup (60 g) raw cashews
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) water
  • 1/2 cup (6 g) cilantro leaves
  • Zest of 1 lime
  • 3 tablespoons (45 ml) fresh lime juice
  • 1 clove garlic, peeled
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt

Peppers:

  • 1 cup (235 ml) vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup (90 g) sprouted dry quinoa
  • 2 tablespoons (30 ml) avocado oil
  • 1/2 cup (71 g) finely chopped sweet onion
  • 1/2 cup (71 g) finely chopped peeled carrots
  • 1/2 cup (71 g) finely chopped celery
  • 1/2 cup (1 1/4 oz [35 g]) finely chopped button mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup (14.5 oz [411 g]) diced tomatoes, with their juice
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 4 cups (2 oz [57 g]) baby spinach
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 large red or yellow bell peppers

Preparation

  1. To make the cashew cream: Add cashews to a medium bowl and cover with boiling water. Soak for 1 hour, drain, and rinse.
  2. Add the soaked cashews to a high-speed blender. Add the water, cilantro, lime zest and juice, garlic, garlic powder, and salt. Blend on high until smooth and creamy. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the chopped greens, chopped herbs, and 1/4 cup (60 ml) of the olive oil, salt, and pepper. Massage together to gently wilt the greens, then set aside.
  4. To make the peppers: Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C, or gas mark 6)
  5. In a small saucepan over medium heat, add the vegetable broth and quinoa. Bring to a boil, cover, reduce heat, and gently simmer for 10 to 12 minutes, until the quinoa is light and fluffy, all the broth is absorbed, and the kernels pop open. Turn off the heat and allow to sit for 5 minutes. Remove lid and fluff quinoa with a fork.
  6. Meanwhile, in a large saute pan over medium heat, add the avocado oil. Once hot, add the onions, carrots, celery, and mushrooms. Saute, stirring occasionally, for 5 to 7 minutes, or until tender.
  7. Stir in the tomatoes and garlic.
  8. Add the spinach and cook, stirring occasionally, for 3 to 4 minutes, or until the spinachis wilted. Turn off the heat, add the quinoa, and stir to combine. Season with salt and pepper.
  9. While the vegetables are cooking, prepare the peppers. Cut the peppers in half lengthwise. Remove and discard the seeds and membranes.
  10. Fill the peppers with the quinoa-spinach mixture and place in a 3-quart (2.8 L) rectangular baking dish. Cover with a tight-fitting lid or foil.
  11. Transfer to the oven and bake for 30 minutes. Uncover and cook an additional 5 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the topping is browned and bubbly.
  12. Remove from the oven and serve with the cashew cream.
